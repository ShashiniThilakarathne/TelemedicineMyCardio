Literature Survey

Sri Lanka is ranked 106th in the world for heart disease.

Figure 1 illustrates leading causes for deaths in last8 years in Sri Lanka. It can be clearly seen that the first leading causefor deaths is cardiovascular diseases. When compare with other causes number of deaths due to cardiovascular diseases significantly high.

The underlying causes of the rise in CVD prevalence, mortality, and burden include social, economic, and cultural changes brought about by globalization and urbanization [1]. Tobacco use, unhealthy food, low level of physical activity, Stress and harmful alcohol use gradually manifest as intermediate risk factors such as raised BP, raised blood glucose, dyslipidaemia, and high body mass index (BMI) [1]. Addressing these risk factors effectively can also help control diseases other than CVDs, such as diabetes, kidney disease, and cancer.

Recent research has employed machine learning techniques to analyze and predict these risk factors. Machine learning algorithms can process large datasets to identify patterns and correlations that traditional statistical methods may miss. For instance, machine learning models have been used to predict the likelihood of heart attacks by analyzing patient data including lifestyle factors, medical history, and genetic information [2]. Such models can help in early diagnosis and personalized treatment plans, potentially reducing the incidence of cardiovascular diseases. Studies have shown that integrating machine learning with clinical practices improves the accuracy of risk assessments and helps in the effective management of preventive measures [3].

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem



How can we reduce deaths from heart failure in Sri Lanka?

To reduce deaths from heart failure in Sri Lanka, improving healthcare access and awareness is crucial. This includes enhancing availability of heart health services for screening, diagnosis, and treatment throughout the country. Public education campaigns are vital to educate people about heart disease risks, symptoms, and the importance of early intervention. Promoting healthier lifestyles through initiatives focused on balanced diets, regular exercise, and smoking cessation can significantly contribute to prevention efforts. Strengthening healthcare infrastructure and providing ongoing training to healthcare professionals will enhance the capability to manage and treat heart failure effectively. By implementing these strategies comprehensively, Sri Lanka can make substantial progress in reducing mortality rates associated with heart failure and improving overall cardiovascular health across its population.

Proposed Solution

Our proposed system integrates four machine learning algorithms designed to enhance heart failure management in Sri Lanka. Each algorithm addresses critical aspects of cardiovascular health: early heart attack identification, wound healing prediction, stress level prediction, and ECG classification. Early heart attack identification focuses on preemptively detecting signs that precede heart failure, enabling timely intervention. Wound healing prediction leverages machine learning to forecast healing rates, crucial for managing complications in cardiac patients. Stress prediction utilizes algorithms to assess stress levels, a significant factor in heart health management. ECG classification enhances diagnostic accuracy by automating the interpretation of electrocardiograms. By leveraging these algorithms and their respective features, our system aims to improve early detection, treatment efficacy, and overall outcomes in heart failure patients across Sri Lanka's healthcare landscape.

Methodology

Our research methodology integrates four distinct machine learning algorithms, each tailored to specific features aimed at reducing heart failure deaths in Sri Lanka. For early heart attack risk prediction, we employ a Random Forest model to analyze patient data and identify potential risks, enabling proactive medical intervention. The wound healing prediction utilizes an Artificial Neural Network (ANN) model to forecast healing rates, essential for managing cardiac patients' recovery processes. Stress prediction leverages a Natural Language Processing (NLP) Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) model to evaluate stress levels from patient-reported data, given the significant impact of stress on heart health. Lastly, an ECG classifier using a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model automates the interpretation of electrocardiograms, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and speed. By integrating these advanced machine learning techniques, our methodology aims to improve early detection, treatment precision, and overall patient outcomes in managing heart failure.
